How to remove red wine stains from cotton

Best first move: blot the stain, flush from the back with cold water, then treat with a detergent solution. If a tint remains, use an oxygen-based bleach (color-safe) and wash as warm as the care label allows.

Works best on fresh stains. For dried stains, see notes below.

Step-by-step

  1. Blot (don’t rub): press with a clean cloth or paper towel to absorb as much wine as possible.
  2. Cold rinse from the back: run cold water through the fabric from the underside to push wine out, not deeper in.
  3. Pre-treat: apply a small amount of liquid laundry detergent (or a mild dishwashing liquid) and gently work it in.
  4. Wait: let it sit for 5–10 minutes. Keep the area damp; don’t let it dry.
  5. Rinse and check: rinse with cold water and reassess. Repeat pre-treatment if needed.
  6. De-tint (if needed): soak in oxygen-based bleach solution per product directions (often 30–60 minutes).
  7. Wash: wash with a heavy-duty detergent at the warmest temperature the care label allows.
  8. Air-dry first: heat from a dryer can set remaining discoloration. Only tumble dry once the stain is gone.

What not to do

  • Don’t rub aggressively — it spreads the stain and roughens fibers.
  • Don’t use hot water first — it can make the stain harder to remove.
  • Don’t put it in the dryer until you’re sure the stain is gone.
  • Don’t use chlorine bleach on colored cotton unless the label explicitly allows it.

Notes for dried stains

If the stain has dried, start with a 15–30 minute soak in cool water with detergent, then follow the steps above. You may need multiple cycles. Patience usually beats harsh chemicals, especially on dyed fabrics.
