Methodology

Our guides are structured to be easy to verify and safe to follow.

Core principles

  • Act fast: fresh stains are easier than set stains.
  • Cold first for proteins: heat can set protein-based stains (e.g., blood, dairy).
  • Blot, don’t rub: rubbing can spread stains and damage fibers.
  • Match method to fabric: delicates and dyes need gentler approaches.
  • Rinse thoroughly: residue can attract soil and cause discoloration.

How to use each guide

  1. Identify the stain type and fabric.
  2. Follow the “answer-first” recommendation at the top.
  3. Use the step-by-step method, then check the “what not to do” section.
  4. When in doubt, choose the gentler method and repeat rather than increasing harshness.

Safety notes

  • Always spot-test first.
  • Never mix chemicals (especially bleach + acids/ammonia).
  • Follow care labels and local product instructions.
Educational guidance only. Always test on an inconspicuous area first.
